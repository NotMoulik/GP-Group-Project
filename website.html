Gilli Danda (Indian Stick and Ball Game)

Gilli Danda is an ancient sport similar to cricket. It is played with two sticks – a long stick (Danda) and a smaller stick (Gilli). The goal is to strike the Gilli with the Danda and send it flying while fielders attempt to catch it or prevent it from traveling far. This game requires precision, hand-eye coordination, and agility.

Pithu (Lagori)

Pithu, also known as Lagori, is a team-based game where players stack flat stones and try to knock them over with a ball. The opposing team attempts to rebuild the stack while avoiding being hit by the ball. This game promotes teamwork, quick reflexes, and strategic planning.

Stapoo (Hopscotch)

Stapoo, or Hopscotch, is a traditional game played by drawing numbered squares on the ground. Players take turns hopping on one leg across the squares while maintaining balance. This game enhances coordination, focus, and endurance.

Kite Flying (Patang Bazi)

Kite Flying, or Patang Bazi, is a vibrant outdoor activity, especially popular during festivals like Basant Panchami and Makar Sankranti. Participants compete to keep their kites in the air while attempting to cut opponents' kites using sharp string-coated thread.

Kancha (Marbles)

Kancha is a childhood favorite where players use small glass marbles to strike others out of a drawn circle. Precision, aim, and strategy are key to winning. This game has been a staple of traditional Indian street play for generations.
